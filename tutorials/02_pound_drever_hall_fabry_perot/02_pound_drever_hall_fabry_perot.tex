\noindent
\textbf{Disadvantages of Pound-Drever-Hall Locking}\\
Previously we investigated \textit{dither locking} as a method of sensing the resonance of a Fabry-P\'erot cavity.
Dither locking is a powerful technique for sensing the resonance of a cavity, 
far better than \textit{half-fringe locking} where the cavity was held at half-power in transmission.

Still, dither locking has some disadvantages.
First, it required a constant length modulation of the cavity, 
which can cause some nonlinearities, 
uses up actuation range, 
limits us to actuating at low frequencies ($< 100~\mathrm{kHz}$),
and allows noise couplings through our system.

Additionally, if we carefully analyze our error signal, 
the zero point may not be exactly at resonance.  
A good cavity resonance error signal should tell us two things: 
\begin{enumerate}
    \item How far away we are from true resonance ($\phi = 0$),
    \item Which direction we need to move to get to resonance ($\phi > 0$ or $\phi < 0$).
\end{enumerate}
If we have a overcoupled cavity, as we often do in practice, the dither lock may take us to just slightly off resonance.

Above we have a diagram of a Fabry-P\'erot cavity set up for Pound-Drever-Hall locking.
Recall the FP cavity reflection equation is 
\begin{align}
r(\phi) = \dfrac{E_\mathrm{refl}}{E_\mathrm{in}}(\phi) = \dfrac{r_1 - r_2 e^{-2 i \phi}}{1 - r_1 r_2 e^{-2 i \phi}}
\end{align} 

\noindent
\textbf{Pound-Drever-Hall Locking}\\
Pound-Drever-Hall circumvents some of these problems by imposing \textit{phase-modulated radio-frequency (RF) sidebands} which co-propagate with the carrier.
These sidebands, labeled \textcolor{BurntOrange}{$\omega_0 + \Omega$} and \textcolor{WildStrawberry}{$\omega_0 - \Omega$} above, are typically tuned such that if the carrier frequency $\omega_0$ resonates in the cavity, the RF sidebands are \textit{antiresonant} in the cavity.

We will explore the setup of the PDH lock in this note.
I recommend using Mathematica, sympy, or Desmos to make some of these plots at the end.

\noindent
\textbf{Derivation of the PDH error signal}

Suppose we start with our carrier light $E_\mathrm{in} = E_0 e^{i \omega_0 t}$.
Then, we send this light through the electro-optic modulator (EOM) to create the phase-modulated sidebands with modulation depth $\Gamma$ and modulation frequency $\Omega$.\\
\textcolor{Mahogany}{\textbf{1. What is the total electric field incident on the front of the cavity?}} \\ 
(It can be helpful to explicitly label your carrier, upper sideband, and lower sideband in the total field).

\vspace{0.5cm}

Next, the light incident on the cavity enters and is reflected by the cavity.
Each of our three fields will interact with the cavity differently.\\
\textcolor{Mahogany}{\textbf{2. Write down three single-pass phases $\phi_0, \phi_\mathrm{usb}, \phi_\mathrm{lsb}$ that the carrier, upper sideband, and lower sideband, will experience as they traverse the cavity, in terms of the cavity length $L$, and carrier and RF frequencies $\omega_0$ and $\Omega$.}}

\vspace{0.5cm}

Now that we have our phases, let's use them to describe the total light that is reflected by the cavity.
\textcolor{Mahogany}{\textbf{3. Write down the cavity total reflected field $E_\mathrm{refl}$ in terms of the reflection function $r(\phi)$ and $\phi_0, \phi_\mathrm{usb}, \phi_\mathrm{lsb}$.}}

\vspace{0.5cm}

Now we have our total reflected field, let's make our power measurement on our photodetector.\\
\textcolor{Mahogany}{\textbf{4. Write down the cavity total reflected power $P_\mathrm{refl}(t)$ in terms of the reflection function $r(\phi)$ and $\phi_0, \phi_\mathrm{usb}, \phi_\mathrm{lsb}$.}}\\
You should end up with nine terms, since you are combining three fields.
At the end, you can set $\Gamma^2 = 0$, to assume small modulation depth.

\vspace{0.5cm}

We have our long $P_\mathrm{refl}(t)$ term.  
Look at the terms which oscillate at 1$\Omega$.
Group the $e^{i \Omega t}$ and $e^{-i \Omega t}$ terms.  
Set the coefficient for $e^{-i \Omega t}$ equal to some complex number $z$,
and the coefficient for $e^{i \Omega t}$ equal to $y$.\\
\textcolor{Mahogany}{\textbf{5. What is $z$ and $y$?  Is there any connection between $z$ and $y$?  What is it?}}

\vspace{0.5cm}
\noindent
\textcolor{Mahogany}{\textbf{6. Rewrite $P_\mathrm{refl}$ in terms of $z$ and $y$.}}

\vspace{0.5cm}

Let's try our demodulation in a new way.
We usually find $P^I$ and $P^Q$ by integrating $P(t)$ over one cycle of $\cos(\Omega t)$ and $\sin(\Omega t)$, because this gives us a single real number for our two quadratures and is how these measurements are usually made. 
But we can combine these equations into a single demodulation over $e^{i \Omega t}$ to recover our 1$\Omega$ demodulated response function $P^{\Omega}(\phi, \phi_\mathrm{usb}, \phi_\mathrm{lsb})$.\\
\textcolor{Mahogany}{\textbf{7. Show we can rewrite the demodulation in this way.}}

\vspace{0.5cm}
\noindent
\textcolor{Mahogany}{\textbf{8. Calculate the 1$\Omega$ demodulated power $P^\Omega_\mathrm{refl}(\phi_0, \phi_\mathrm{usb}, \phi_\mathrm{lsb})$.  What can we see pops out?}}

\vspace{0.5cm}
\noindent
This term $P^\Omega_\mathrm{refl}$ is our PDH error signal. 
Assume the RF sidebands are antiresonant, so $\phi_\mathrm{usb} = -\phi_\mathrm{lsb} = \dfrac{\pi}{2}$.\\
\textcolor{Mahogany}{\textbf{9. Calculate the derivative of $P^\Omega_\mathrm{refl}$ with respect to $\phi_0$, then evaluate it about zero.}}\\
This is called the \textit{discriminant} or \textit{optical gain} of the error signal, in units of watts per radian.  

\vspace{0.5cm}
\noindent
\textcolor{Mahogany}{\textbf{10. Plot the real and imaginary parts of $P^\Omega_\mathrm{refl}(\phi_0, \phi_\mathrm{usb}, \phi_\mathrm{lsb})$ as a function of $\phi_0 \in [-\pi, \pi]$.\\
Plot the discriminant $\dfrac{P^\Omega_\mathrm{refl}}{d \phi_0}$ about $\phi_0 = 0$ for comparison.}}\\
Choose a moderate finesse cavity with length $L = 1~\mathrm{m}, T_1 = T_2 = 20\%$, and $\Gamma = 0.1~\mathrm{radians}$.\\
State what the modulation frequency $\Omega$ must be to be exactly antiresonant in the $L = 1~\mathrm{m}$ cavity.

\vspace{0.5cm}
\noindent
\textcolor{Mahogany}{\textbf{11. Plot $P^\Omega_\mathrm{refl}(\phi_0, \phi_\mathrm{usb}, \phi_\mathrm{lsb})$ as a phasor as a function of $\phi_0 \in [-\pi, \pi]$.}}\\
What shape do you get?
